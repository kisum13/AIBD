# Fixed Fee Negotiation Policy

> Internal pricing and negotiation rules for Fixed Project Fee conversations.

## 1. Core Principle

Fixed Fee is a one-time project fee, not per video or per post.

When creators compare it to normal brand campaign rates, explain that this is an AI short drama project with platform-side production and distribution.

## 2. Do Not Proactively Increase Fixed Fee

Do not say:

- We can increase the fixed fee.
- We can match your rate.
- We can add extra budget.
- We can pay per post.

Use:

> The fixed fee is based on our internal evaluation and current project budget.

If the user gives a specific approved maximum, use that number.

## 3. Low Fixed Fee Handling

If the fixed fee is low:

Do not say:

- Your views are bad.
- Your account performance is poor.
- We can only pay a very small amount.

Use:

> Our fixed-fee budget is based on a standardized internal evaluation, including recent account performance, audience engagement, and overall project fit.

Then recommend Revenue Share.

## 4. High Rate Card Handling

When creator sends a high rate card:

1. Thank them.
2. Acknowledge their value.
3. Explain this is not a traditional sponsored post.
4. State approved maximum fixed fee if provided by user.
5. Recommend Revenue Share.

Example:

> We completely understand your standard rates. This project is structured differently from a traditional brand campaign, as our team handles the AI short drama production, platform release, and global distribution. The highest fixed project fee we can currently offer is [amount] for the entire project. That said, I would strongly recommend Revenue Share because it offers greater long-term upside.

## 5. No Hybrid by Default

Do not offer fixed fee + revenue share unless explicitly approved by user.

Default reply:

> At the moment, our standard model supports either Fixed Project Fee or Revenue Share, rather than a combination of both.

## 6. Payment Schedule Guardrail

Do not say:

- 100% upfront
- 50% immediately upon signing
- Payment before materials/script/production

Correct:

> The first payment is made within 10 business days after the agreement is signed, the script is confirmed, required materials are provided, and production officially begins. The second payment is made within 10 business days after official online release and verification.

## 7. Contract Term Guardrail

Do not proactively offer shorter terms.

If creator asks:

> The attached agreement reflects our current standard terms. If the contract term becomes a key concern as we move toward finalizing the collaboration, I can check internally whether there is any flexibility.

## 8. When to Recommend Revenue Share

Recommend Revenue Share when:

- Creator asks for higher fixed fee.
- Creator’s rate card is above budget.
- Creator has strong audience size or engagement.
- Creator seems interested in long-term income.
- Fixed fee is too low to be attractive.

Do not recommend aggressively when:

- Creator already accepted fixed fee.
- Creator is reviewing final contract.
- Creator only asks procedural payment questions.

## 9. Best Fixed Fee Reply Structure

1. Acknowledge creator’s rate / concern.
2. Explain project difference.
3. State fixed fee limit if approved.
4. Clarify this is entire project fee.
5. Explain payment schedule if asked.
6. Recommend Revenue Share if appropriate.
7. Offer to send the relevant agreement.
