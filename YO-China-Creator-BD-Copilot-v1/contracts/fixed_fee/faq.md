# Fixed Fee Agreement FAQ

> Internal FAQ for answering creator questions about the Fixed Project Fee model.

---

## Q1. Is the fixed fee paid per video?

No. The fixed fee is for the entire project.

Recommended reply:

> The Fixed Project Fee is for the entire AI short drama project, not a payment per individual video or per post.

---

## Q2. What does the fixed fee cover?

It covers the creator’s participation in the AI short drama project and all related promotional obligations under the agreement.

Recommended reply:

> The fixed fee covers the full project collaboration, including your participation in the AI short drama and the agreed promotional content related to the project.

---

## Q3. How many promotional posts are required?

At least five promotional contents for the same project.

Recommended reply:

> Creators usually publish around five promotional contents during the launch period, such as free episodes, trailers, highlights, behind-the-scenes content, or other agreed promotional materials.

---

## Q4. Are the five promotional posts five separate campaigns?

No.

Recommended reply:

> No. The five promotional contents are all part of the same AI short drama project and do not count as five separate commercial campaigns.

---

## Q5. Do I get extra payment for each promotional post?

No under the standard fixed-fee agreement.

Recommended reply:

> The Fixed Project Fee already covers all agreed promotional obligations for the project, so there is no additional payment based on the number or format of promotional posts.

---

## Q6. When is the first fixed-fee payment made?

The first payment is made within 10 business days after agreement signing, script confirmation, creator materials provision, and production commencement.

Recommended reply:

> The first payment is made within 10 business days after the agreement is signed, the necessary materials are provided, the script is confirmed, and the project officially enters production.

---

## Q7. Is there any payment immediately upon signing?

Not automatically. The first payment requires the relevant contract milestones to be met.

Recommended reply:

> The first payment is not simply triggered by signing alone. It is made after the agreement is signed, required materials are provided, the script is confirmed, and production officially begins.

---

## Q8. When is the second payment made?

Within 10 business days after official online release and verification.

Recommended reply:

> The second payment is made within 10 business days after the AI short drama is officially released online and the release conditions are verified.

---

## Q9. Can the full payment be made upfront?

Default: no.

Recommended reply:

> Our standard Fixed Project Fee model follows a two-installment payment schedule. We are not able to provide full upfront payment under the standard agreement.

---

## Q10. What currency is payment made in?

USD unless otherwise agreed.

Recommended reply:

> Payment is generally made in USD unless otherwise agreed by both parties.

---

## Q11. What if payment is made in another currency?

Exchange rate on payment date applies, and cross-border fees may be included in project costs.

Recommended reply:

> If payment needs to be made in another currency, conversion will be based on the exchange rate on the payment date, and cross-border processing fees may apply.

---

## Q12. Can I negotiate a higher fixed fee?

Do not promise increase.

Recommended reply:

> The fixed fee is based on our internal evaluation and current project budget. If you are looking for stronger upside, I would recommend considering the Revenue Share model instead.

---

## Q13. Why is the fixed fee lower than my usual brand rate?

Recommended reply:

> This collaboration is different from a traditional sponsored post. It is a project-based AI short drama where our team handles story development, AI production, editing, localization, distribution, and platform operation. The fixed fee is based on our internal evaluation for the full project.

---

## Q14. Can we do fixed fee + revenue share?

Default: no hybrid.

Recommended reply:

> At the moment, our standard collaboration model supports either a Fixed Project Fee or Revenue Share, rather than a combination of both.

---

## Q15. Will my image be used for AI training?

No.

Recommended reply:

> No. Your likeness authorization is limited to this specific AI short drama project and related promotion/distribution. It does not authorize general-purpose AI model training.

---

## Q16. Can my image be used for ads or brand collaborations?

Not under this agreement unless separately agreed.

Recommended reply:

> No. Under this agreement, your likeness is used only for this specific AI short drama project and related promotion/distribution. Any independent advertising, livestream e-commerce, or brand collaboration would require a separate agreement.

---

## Q17. Can I review the script?

Yes, primarily for image protection concerns.

Recommended reply:

> Yes. You may review the script mainly to ensure that the content does not negatively affect your public image, reputation, or commercial goodwill.

---

## Q18. Can I require script changes?

Creator may submit reasonable comments related to image protection. Platform retains final creative control.

Recommended reply:

> You can submit comments if you believe the script affects your image or reputation. Our team will review your feedback in good faith, while the platform retains final creative decision-making authority.

---

## Q19. Can I preview the final work before release?

Yes. The creator may preview the completed final work before release.

Recommended reply:

> Yes, you will be able to preview the completed work before final release and submit reasonable comments regarding whether the use of your authorized image complies with the agreement.

---

## Q20. Who owns the final AI drama?

Party A / platform owns the project IP.

Recommended reply:

> The platform owns the intellectual property rights in the final AI short drama and related project materials. Your personal likeness rights remain yours, but the project works themselves are owned and operated by the platform.

---

## Q21. Can I post the AI drama on my own social accounts?

Yes, agreed promotional content may be posted free of charge according to the project plan.

Recommended reply:

> Yes. You will publish agreed promotional content on your social media accounts, such as free episodes, trailers, or highlights, according to the promotional schedule.

---

## Q22. Can I monetize the posted content myself?

No independent monetization unless separately agreed.

Recommended reply:

> Creator-side posts are for project promotion and audience guidance. The full commercial operation of the project is handled by the platform and authorized channels.

---

## Q23. How long does the official link need to stay?

At least 60 consecutive days from first public release.

Recommended reply:

> The official tracking link should remain accessible for at least 60 consecutive days after first public release so that revenue and performance can be tracked accurately.

---

## Q24. Where should the official link be placed?

Profile, homepage, pinned post, comment anchor, or other platform-permitted placement.

Recommended reply:

> The link can be placed in your profile, homepage, pinned post, pinned comment, or another agreed location allowed by the platform.

---

## Q25. Can the contract term be shortened?

Do not proactively agree.

Recommended reply:

> The attached agreement reflects our current standard terms. If we move toward the final stage of the collaboration and the contract term becomes a key concern, I’d be happy to discuss internally whether there is any flexibility.

---

## Q26. Can I work with other AI companies?

Yes, for unrelated projects. Avoid blanket exclusivity.

Recommended reply:

> The agreement is not intended to prevent you from working with other AI companies in general. The restriction is project-specific and mainly protects the AI character, role setting, and project concept created through this collaboration.

---

## Q27. Why is there a non-compete clause?

Recommended reply:

> The purpose is to protect the uniqueness and commercial value of the AI character and project we create together, not to restrict unrelated AI or brand collaborations.

---

## Q28. Can I request sales or revenue verification?

Yes, once every two months.

Recommended reply:

> Yes. The agreement allows you to request verification of sales data and Net Revenue once every two months.

---

## Q29. Does fixed fee mean I do not receive revenue share?

Yes, unless separately agreed.

Recommended reply:

> Under the Fixed Project Fee model, your compensation is the agreed fixed amount. Revenue Share is a separate collaboration model.

---

## Q30. Which model do you recommend?

If creator has strong audience, recommend Revenue Share.

Recommended reply:

> If you prefer guaranteed compensation, the Fixed Project Fee model may be suitable. However, if you are open to long-term upside, I would honestly recommend the Revenue Share model, especially for creators with strong audience engagement.
