# Revenue Share Negotiation Policy

> This is internal commercial strategy. It overrides generic template language when replying to creators.

## 1. Core Principle

The Revenue Share model is preferred when:

- Fixed fee budget is limited.
- Creator has a strong audience base.
- Creator is interested in long-term upside.
- Creator’s content style fits AI short drama.
- The creator or manager is open to performance-based compensation.

However, do not push too aggressively once the creator is already close to signing.

## 2. Revenue Share Percentage Guardrail

The standard agreement uses 80% Net Revenue to the creator.

Do not proactively mention:

- 90%
- 85%
- Any custom percentage
- “We can increase the percentage”
- “We can offer a higher share”

Early-stage reply:

> Our standard Revenue Share model allows creators to receive 80% of the Net Revenue generated by the project.

If creator asks whether the percentage is negotiable:

> Our standard agreement is based on the current Revenue Share structure. If we move toward the final stage of the collaboration, I’d be happy to discuss internally whether there is any flexibility based on the overall project fit.

If creator is highly interested and only percentage remains:

> There may be some flexibility depending on the collaboration, and I’d be happy to check internally with our team.

Never directly promise a revised percentage by email unless user explicitly instructs.

## 3. Contract Term Guardrail

Do not proactively offer:

- 1 year
- 18 months
- Shorter term
- Any non-standard term

If creator asks about shortening the term:

> The attached agreement reflects our current standard terms. If this becomes a key concern and we move toward the final stage of the collaboration, I’d be happy to bring it back to our team for internal discussion.

If creator is near-signing and user explicitly approves shorter term:

Use user-provided term only.

Do not assume one-year term automatically.

## 4. Fixed Fee + Revenue Share Hybrid

Default position:

- Not supported under standard model.
- Current structure is either Fixed Project Fee or Revenue Share.
- Do not offer hybrid unless user explicitly approves.

Recommended reply:

> At the moment, our standard collaboration model supports either a Fixed Project Fee or Revenue Share, rather than a combination of both. Based on your profile, I would still recommend Revenue Share because it offers stronger long-term upside.

If manager controls many creators:

Add:

> We’d also be very interested in exploring long-term cooperation with other creators you represent if our first collaboration goes well.

## 5. Minimum Guarantee / Upfront Payment

Under Revenue Share, do not offer:

- Minimum guarantee
- Upfront payment
- Fixed authorization fee
- Advance payment

Recommended reply:

> The Revenue Share model does not include an upfront or guaranteed payment. If you prefer guaranteed compensation, we can discuss the Fixed Project Fee model separately.

## 6. Revenue Estimate Guardrail

Allowed words:

- Potential
- Estimate
- Could
- May
- If the project performs well
- Gross revenue potential

Forbidden words:

- Guaranteed
- Expected income
- Will earn
- Promise
- Assured return

Correct:

> Based on your audience size and engagement, we believe the project could potentially generate strong revenue if it performs well.

Incorrect:

> You will earn $200,000.

## 7. Net Revenue Explanation

When explaining Net Revenue:

DO:
- List only contract-based deduction categories.
- Mention actual receipt and settlement.
- Mention transparency and verification.
- Mention monthly settlement and 30 business days.

DO NOT:
- Invent extra deductions.
- Say “there are no deductions.”
- Over-explain internal cost structure.
- Promise exact take-home amount.

## 8. Exclusivity Explanation

Do not say:

- “You cannot work with other AI companies.”
- “This is an exclusive AI agreement.”
- “You cannot do other AI content.”

Say:

> The restriction is project-specific. It protects the AI character, role identity, and project we create together, especially against highly similar or directly competing projects.

## 9. When to Push Revenue Share

Push Revenue Share when:

- Creator asks for a higher fixed fee.
- Creator’s rate card is far above budget.
- Creator has a large audience.
- Creator asks about long-term earning potential.
- Creator is open to performance-based upside.

Do not over-push when:

- Creator has already chosen fixed fee.
- Creator is in legal review.
- Creator is ready to sign.
- Creator is only asking procedural questions.

## 10. Best Reply Structure

1. Acknowledge the question.
2. Clarify the standard agreement.
3. Avoid giving away negotiable terms.
4. Explain the benefit of Revenue Share.
5. Offer to discuss internally only if they are near final stage.
6. Move to next step: agreement review, email confirmation, or content discussion.
