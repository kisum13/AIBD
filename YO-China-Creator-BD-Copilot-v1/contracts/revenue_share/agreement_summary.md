# Revenue Share Agreement Summary

> Internal AI-readable summary. Use this for replying to creator questions about the Revenue Share agreement. Do not send this file directly to creators.

## 1. Cooperation Model

The Revenue Share Agreement is used when the creator chooses a revenue-sharing collaboration model instead of a fixed project fee.

Under this model:

- Party A / YourChannel is responsible for script creation, AI content production, post-production editing, multilingual localization, platform operations, content distribution, and project management.
- Party B / Creator authorizes the use of their portrait, image, voice, and other relevant personality rights within the scope of this specific AI short drama project.
- The creator assists in promoting the collaborative work through their social media channels.
- The creator does not receive a fixed authorization fee, guaranteed fee, advance payment, minimum revenue guarantee, or one-time collaboration fee under the standard Revenue Share agreement.

## 2. Content Scope

The collaboration covers at least one AI-generated film / television / short drama work.

Each work shall consist of no fewer than ten episodes under the agreement.

In external emails, use softer language:

> A complete AI short drama usually consists of around 10–20 episodes.

Do not over-explain minimum episode wording unless the creator asks about the contract.

## 3. Story and Script Workflow

The platform may provide story outline options for creator selection.

The creator may select a project direction / foundational outline.

After a direction is confirmed:

- The platform independently develops the script.
- The platform has final decision-making authority over scriptwriting, dialogue design, character settings, plot development, ending arrangements, and overall creative direction.
- The creator may review the script primarily for image protection concerns.

## 4. Image Protection

The project script and related content should not intentionally distort, defame, slander, or materially harm the creator's public image, reputation, or commercial goodwill.

The content should not include:

- Pornography
- Gambling
- Drugs
- Illegal violence
- Hate, discrimination, or unlawful conduct
- Content clearly violating public order or good morals

If the creator believes the script contains any of the above concerns, they should provide written comments within the review period stated in the agreement.

Email wording should be reassuring:

> You will have the opportunity to review the story/script for image protection concerns before production moves forward.

Avoid implying that the creator has full creative veto power.

## 5. Authorization Scope

Creator authorization is limited to this specific collaborative AI project.

Authorized materials may include:

- Portrait / likeness
- Image
- Facial features
- Body shape / body characteristics
- Voice features
- Other relevant personality rights

This authorization is used to generate character performance content for the confirmed AI work.

## 6. What Authorization Does NOT Include

Do not imply broad or unlimited use.

The authorization does NOT mean:

- General-purpose AI model training
- Independent advertising endorsements
- Livestream e-commerce
- Brand collaborations
- Unrelated commercial campaigns
- Unrelated AI projects

If such additional uses are needed, they should require separate agreement.

Recommended external wording:

> Your likeness will only be used for this specific AI short drama project and its related promotion and distribution. It does not authorize general AI model training or unrelated commercial use.

## 7. Distribution Rights

The platform may distribute, broadcast, publish, promote, commercially operate, and monetize the collaborative works globally through online video platforms and other media channels.

External wording:

> The series will be distributed and promoted globally through our platform and authorized channels.

Avoid over-expanding unless the creator asks about legal distribution scope.

## 8. Creator Promotion Obligations

The creator should make reasonable commercial efforts to promote the collaborative works through social media and guide fans to the YourChannel platform.

Promotion methods may include:

- Homepage/profile link placement
- Promotional video publication
- Story or moments posts
- Trailer and highlight clip publication
- Pinning official links in comments
- Other reasonable and lawful promotional activities

## 9. Five Promotional Contents

The creator shall produce and publish no fewer than five promotional content items for the same collaborative project, according to the promotional plan.

Important explanation:

- These five items are all part of the same project promotion.
- They do not constitute five separate commercial collaborations.
- They do not create five separate authorizations.
- They do not require additional payment beyond the agreed compensation model.

External wording:

> Around five promotional posts are usually required during the launch period, including free episodes, trailers, highlight clips, behind-the-scenes materials, or other mutually agreed promotional content. These are all part of the same AI short drama project, not separate brand campaigns.

## 10. Official Tracking Link

The creator should place the official tracking link provided by the platform on their social media profile/homepage/comment/pinned area as permitted by the platform.

The link should remain accessible for at least 60 consecutive days from the first public release of the relevant work.

External wording:

> To ensure accurate revenue tracking, we will provide an official tracking link. The link is typically placed in the creator's profile, pinned post, comment, or another agreed position for the launch period.

## 11. Revenue Share Ratio

The standard agreement states that the creator receives 80% of Net Revenue.

Party A retains 20% of Net Revenue for platform service fees, content production costs, operation management fees, distribution support fees, and related business costs.

Important internal rule:

- Do not proactively offer 90%, 85%, or any custom percentage.
- Do not say “we can increase the percentage” in early-stage email replies.
- If the creator is highly interested and the only remaining blocker is share percentage, say:
  > There may be some flexibility depending on the collaboration, and I’d be happy to discuss it internally with our team.

## 12. Net Revenue Definition

Net Revenue refers to distributable income actually received and settled by Party A from the collaborative works after deducting:

- Payment channel processing fees
- Taxes and levies payable under applicable laws
- Platform service fees
- Refunds
- Chargebacks
- Fraudulent transactions and similar deductions
- Fees required by third-party platforms or cooperative channels
- Other reasonable expenses recognized by both parties or permitted by applicable laws and regulations

Only income actually received and settled by Party A is included in the revenue-sharing calculation.

## 13. Revenue Settlement

Revenue is calculated on a calendar-month basis.

The platform provides the creator with the corresponding revenue settlement statement and completes payment within 30 business days after the end of each settlement cycle.

Payment is made in USD unless otherwise agreed.

If payment is made in another currency, conversion may follow the bank exchange rate on the payment date, and cross-border handling fees may be included in project costs.

## 14. Payment Condition

The platform’s obligation to pay revenue share arises only after the corresponding income has actually been received and settled by Party A and entered Party A’s designated bank account.

Party A has no obligation to prepay, advance, or make early revenue-sharing payments before actual receipt of revenue.

External wording:

> Revenue share payments are made after the platform has actually received and settled the corresponding project revenue.

## 15. No Revenue Guarantee

The Revenue Share model does not include:

- Fixed authorization fee
- Guaranteed fee
- Advance payment
- Minimum revenue guarantee
- One-time collaboration fee

Party A does not guarantee:

- View count
- Audience numbers
- Market performance
- Profitability
- Revenue level
- Commercial success

If the collaborative works do not generate actual revenue, Party A has no obligation to pay fees under the Revenue Share model.

External wording:

> Revenue Share offers upside potential, but it is not a guaranteed payment model. Actual performance depends on story quality, audience engagement, promotion, and market response.

## 16. Revenue Verification

The creator has the right to request verification of sales data and Net Revenue once every two months.

External wording:

> To ensure transparency, creators may request verification of sales data and Net Revenue according to the agreement.

If discussing platform dashboard, use:

> We will also provide access to platform-side data where available, so the creator can track relevant project performance and revenue information.

## 17. Intellectual Property

All copyrights, neighboring rights, intellectual property rights, and other rights in the works produced under the agreement belong to Party A.

This includes:

- AI film and television works
- Raw materials
- Character settings
- Scripts
- Dialogue
- Storyboards
- Visual designs
- Digital human image data
- AI-generated intermediate products
- Final works

The creator retains personality rights but does not acquire copyright property rights or ownership of the project IP solely through revenue sharing.

## 18. Creator Personality Rights

The creator’s portrait, image, and related personal rights remain personality rights of the creator.

However, the creator authorizes project-specific use within the scope of the collaboration.

External wording:

> Your personal likeness rights remain yours. The agreement only authorizes us to use your likeness for this specific AI short drama project and its related distribution and promotion.

## 19. Exclusivity / Non-compete Explanation

The agreement contains restrictions designed to protect the specific AI character, role identity, and project created under this collaboration.

Important external wording:

> This is not a blanket restriction on all AI-related collaborations. The restriction is intended to protect highly similar project-specific characters, role settings, or directly competing AI short drama projects based on the same or substantially similar concept.

Avoid saying:

- “You cannot work with other AI companies.”
- “This is an exclusive AI partnership.”
- “You cannot do any AI content.”

## 20. Confidentiality

The creator should keep confidential:

- Full scripts
- Episode summaries
- Character settings
- Unpublished materials
- Plot twists
- Production budgets
- Cost breakdowns
- Schedules
- Marketing strategies
- ROI data
- Contract content
- Revenue sharing ratio
- Project financial statements
- Internal business resources

External wording should be simple:

> Some project materials and contract terms are confidential because they include unreleased scripts, production information, and financial details.

## 21. Breach and Withholding

If the creator fails to complete required link placement, promotion, or other obligations in a way that affects accurate revenue calculation or project operation, Party A may suspend or withhold corresponding revenue sharing until the creator fulfills the relevant obligation.

When replying externally, keep it softer:

> Accurate link placement and agreed promotional activities are important because they allow us to properly track revenue and calculate revenue share.

## 22. Governing Law / Dispute Resolution

The agreement provides for dispute resolution under the stated governing law and arbitration/jurisdiction clause.

Do not proactively discuss dispute resolution in sales emails unless the creator asks.

## 23. Internal Reply Guardrails

When replying to creators:

DO:
- Explain clearly and warmly.
- Emphasize transparency.
- Use contract-based wording.
- Use “potential” and “estimate” for revenue.
- Recommend Revenue Share when fixed fee is limited.
- Clarify project-specific image use.

DO NOT:
- Promise guaranteed earnings.
- Promise 90% or any special share.
- Promise a one-year term.
- Promise upfront payment for Revenue Share.
- Say the creator cannot work with all AI companies.
- Over-explain legal risk unless asked.
