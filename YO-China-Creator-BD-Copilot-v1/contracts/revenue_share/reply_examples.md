# Revenue Share Reply Examples

## 1. Creator asks: What is Net Revenue?

Hi,

Thank you for your question. I’d be happy to clarify.

Net Revenue refers to the revenue actually received and settled by the platform from the project after deducting reasonable project-related costs, such as payment processing fees, applicable taxes, platform service fees, refunds, chargebacks, and third-party platform or channel deductions.

Only revenue that has actually been received and settled by the platform is included in the revenue share calculation.

To ensure transparency, revenue is calculated on a monthly basis, and the corresponding revenue statement and payment are completed according to the settlement schedule in the agreement. You may also request verification of sales data and Net Revenue once every two months.

---

## 2. Creator asks: Is there any guaranteed payment?

Hi,

Under the Revenue Share model, there is no fixed authorization fee, guaranteed fee, advance payment, minimum guarantee, or one-time collaboration fee.

Instead, you receive a share of the Net Revenue generated by the project. This model gives creators the opportunity to benefit from the long-term performance of the series, rather than being limited to a one-time fixed payment.

If you prefer guaranteed compensation, we can also discuss the Fixed Project Fee model separately.

---

## 3. Creator asks: Can you offer 90% revenue share?

Hi,

Thank you for your question. Our standard Revenue Share agreement is based on the current revenue-sharing structure.

If we move further toward the final stage of the collaboration and this becomes a key point for you, I’d be happy to discuss internally with our team whether there is any flexibility based on the overall project fit.

For now, I’d recommend reviewing the standard agreement first so you can better understand the full collaboration structure.

---

## 4. Creator asks: Can we sign for one year instead?

Hi,

Thank you for raising this. The attached agreement reflects our current standard contract terms.

If we move toward the final stage of the collaboration and the contract term becomes a key concern, I’d be happy to bring it back to our team for internal discussion.

For now, I’d suggest reviewing the standard agreement first, and please feel free to let me know if you have any specific concerns.

---

## 5. Creator asks: Can I still work with other AI companies?

Hi,

Yes, you can still work with other companies on other AI-related projects in general.

The agreement is not intended to be a blanket restriction on all AI collaborations. It is project-specific and mainly protects the AI character, role identity, and project concept we create together, especially against highly similar or directly competing AI short drama projects based on the same or substantially similar character or concept.

---

## 6. Creator asks: Will my image be used for AI training?

Hi,

No. Your likeness authorization is limited to this specific AI short drama project and its related promotion and distribution.

It does not authorize general-purpose AI model training, and it does not allow us to use your likeness for unrelated commercial projects, independent advertisements, livestream e-commerce, or brand collaborations without a separate agreement.

---

## 7. Creator asks: How many posts do I need to publish?

Hi,

Creators usually publish around five promotional contents during the launch period. These may include free episodes, trailers, highlight clips, Stories, pinned-link posts, or other mutually agreed promotional materials.

Just to clarify, these are all part of the same AI short drama project promotion. They are not treated as five separate brand campaigns or separate commercial collaborations.

---

## 8. Creator asks: How long does the link need to stay?

Hi,

To ensure accurate revenue tracking, the official tracking link should remain accessible for at least 60 consecutive days after the first public release of the project.

We’ll coordinate with you before launch on where the link should be placed, such as your profile, bio, pinned comment, or another platform-permitted placement.

---

## 9. Creator asks: When do I get paid?

Hi,

Revenue is calculated on a monthly basis. After each settlement cycle, we provide the corresponding revenue statement and complete payment according to the schedule in the agreement.

Please note that revenue share is paid after the platform has actually received and settled the corresponding project revenue.

---

## 10. Creator asks: Can we do fixed fee + revenue share?

Hi,

I completely understand why a hybrid model would be attractive.

At the moment, our standard collaboration structure supports either a Fixed Project Fee or a Revenue Share model, rather than a combination of both.

Based on your profile, I would still recommend the Revenue Share model because it offers stronger long-term upside if the project performs well.

---

## 11. Creator asks: Is there any upfront payment?

Hi,

Under the Revenue Share model, there is no upfront payment or advance payment.

Revenue share is paid after the project generates actual revenue and the corresponding revenue has been received and settled by the platform.

If upfront guaranteed compensation is important to you, we can discuss whether the Fixed Project Fee model would be more suitable.

---

## 12. Creator asks: Can I review the script?

Hi,

Yes. You will have the opportunity to review the story/script mainly for image protection concerns, such as whether the content may negatively affect your public image, reputation, or commercial goodwill.

Our content team will review your feedback in good faith, while the platform retains final creative decision-making authority for the project.
