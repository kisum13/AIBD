# Revenue Share Agreement FAQ

> Internal FAQ for answering creator questions. Use friendly email language. Do not paste the whole FAQ to creators.

---

## Q1. What is the Revenue Share model?

The Revenue Share model means the creator does not receive a one-time fixed payment. Instead, the creator receives a percentage of the Net Revenue generated by the AI short drama project.

Recommended reply:

> Under the Revenue Share model, you receive a percentage of the Net Revenue generated by the AI short drama. This means your earnings are tied to the long-term performance of the series rather than being limited to a one-time fixed payment.

---

## Q2. Is there a fixed fee under the Revenue Share model?

No. The standard Revenue Share agreement does not include a fixed authorization fee, guaranteed fee, advance payment, minimum guarantee, or one-time collaboration fee.

Recommended reply:

> The Revenue Share model does not include a fixed fee or minimum guarantee. If you prefer guaranteed compensation, we can discuss the Fixed Project Fee model separately.

---

## Q3. What percentage does the creator receive?

The standard Revenue Share agreement states that the creator receives 80% of Net Revenue.

Internal guardrail:

Do not proactively offer higher percentages. If the creator is highly interested and this is the final blocker, say there may be some flexibility and that you can check internally.

Recommended reply:

> Under the standard Revenue Share model, creators receive 80% of the Net Revenue generated by the project.

---

## Q4. Can the revenue share percentage be negotiated?

Early-stage reply:

> Our standard Revenue Share structure is based on the current agreement. If we move further toward the final stage of the collaboration, I’d be happy to discuss internally whether there is any flexibility based on the overall project fit.

Do not say:
- We can offer 90%.
- We can increase it.
- We can do 85/15.

---

## Q5. What is Net Revenue?

Net Revenue means the distributable income actually received and settled by the platform from the project after deducting project-related costs and fees.

Typical deductions include:

- Payment processing fees
- Taxes and legally required levies
- Platform service fees
- Refunds
- Chargebacks
- Fraudulent transactions
- Third-party platform or channel deductions
- Other lawful and reasonable expenses recognized by the parties

Recommended reply:

> Net Revenue refers to the revenue actually received and settled by the platform from the project after deducting reasonable and necessary project-related costs, such as payment processing fees, applicable taxes, platform service fees, refunds, chargebacks, and third-party platform deductions.

---

## Q6. Is Net Revenue calculated before or after costs?

It is calculated after deducting the listed costs and fees.

Recommended reply:

> Net Revenue is calculated after the applicable project-related deductions listed in the agreement.

---

## Q7. Can the creator see revenue data?

Yes. Creators may receive platform-side revenue information and may request verification of sales data and Net Revenue once every two months.

Recommended reply:

> To ensure transparency, we will provide project revenue information according to the agreement, and you may request verification of sales data and Net Revenue once every two months.

---

## Q8. How often is revenue settled?

Revenue is calculated on a calendar-month basis.

Payment is completed within 30 business days after the end of each settlement cycle.

Recommended reply:

> Revenue is calculated monthly, and the creator’s share is paid within 30 business days after the end of each settlement cycle.

---

## Q9. When will I receive the money?

The creator receives revenue share after the platform has actually received and settled the relevant project revenue.

Recommended reply:

> Revenue share is paid after the platform has actually received and settled the corresponding project revenue, and payment is completed according to the monthly settlement schedule in the agreement.

---

## Q10. Is there any upfront payment under Revenue Share?

No. The platform does not prepay or advance revenue share before actual receipt of revenue.

Recommended reply:

> Under the Revenue Share model, there is no upfront payment or advance payment. Revenue share is paid after the corresponding project revenue has actually been received and settled.

---

## Q11. Is there a minimum guarantee?

No. The standard Revenue Share model has no minimum revenue guarantee.

Recommended reply:

> The Revenue Share model does not include a minimum guarantee. Its advantage is that it allows creators to benefit from the long-term upside if the project performs well.

---

## Q12. What happens if the project generates no revenue?

If the project generates no actual revenue, there is no payment obligation under the Revenue Share agreement.

Recommended reply:

> If the project does not generate actual revenue, there would be no revenue share payment. That said, our goal is to work together to make the project perform as strongly as possible through both creator promotion and platform distribution.

---

## Q13. Can you guarantee views, subscribers, or revenue?

No. Never guarantee performance.

Recommended reply:

> We cannot guarantee exact views, audience size, profitability, or revenue level. Actual performance depends on the story, audience engagement, promotion, and market response.

---

## Q14. Can I still work with other AI companies?

Yes, generally. The restriction is not a blanket ban on all AI collaborations.

Recommended reply:

> Yes. The agreement is not intended to prevent you from working with other AI companies in general. It only protects the specific AI character, role identity, and project we create together, especially against highly similar or directly competing projects.

---

## Q15. Is this an exclusive AI partnership?

No. Do not call it a full exclusive AI partnership.

Recommended reply:

> This is not a general exclusive AI partnership. It is a project-specific collaboration, and the restrictions mainly apply to highly similar project-specific characters or competing works based on the same concept.

---

## Q16. Will my image be used for general AI model training?

No.

Recommended reply:

---

## Q17. Can my likeness be used for ads, livestream selling, or brand deals?

Not under this agreement unless separately agreed.

Recommended reply:

> No. Under this agreement, your likeness is only used for the specific AI short drama project and related promotion/distribution. Independent advertising endorsements, livestream e-commerce, or brand collaborations would require a separate agreement.

The platform owns the IP and copyright of the project works.

Recommended reply:

Recommended reply:

The creator may submit reasonable comments related to image protection. The platform retains final creative decision-making authority.

Recommended reply:

> You can share comments if you believe the script may negatively affect your image or reputation. Our content team will review your feedback in good faith, while the final creative decisions remain with the platform.

---

The creator may choose from provided story outlines and share creative preferences.

Recommended reply:
