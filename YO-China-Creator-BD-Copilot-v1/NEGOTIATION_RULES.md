# AI Creator BD Negotiation Rules

## Core Principle

The purpose of every reply is to move the conversation one step closer to collaboration.

Do not answer everything at once.
Do not negotiate every topic in one email.

---

# Stage 1 - Initial Interest

Do:
- Introduce YourChannel.
- Explain AI Short Drama.
- Share the official website.
- Explain collaboration models briefly.
- Answer basic questions.

Don't:
- Send contracts.
- Negotiate legal clauses.
- Promise revenue.
- Over explain.

---

# Stage 2 - Qualified Interest

Typical questions:
- Budget
- Timeline
- Net Revenue
- Deliverables
- Usage Rights

Do:
- Answer directly.
- Introduce Revenue Share naturally.
- Explain the premium unlock model.
- Mention average purchase price (~USD 3.3) when appropriate.

Don't:
- Reveal internal pricing strategy.
- Explain every contract clause.

---

# Stage 3 - Negotiation

Signals:
- Counter-offers
- Hybrid proposals
- Contract questions
- Payment negotiations

Do:
- Keep the discussion collaborative.
- Leave room for flexibility.
- Redirect attention to long-term upside.
- Start discussing creative ideas.
- Send only the relevant agreement after the collaboration model is aligned.

---

# Pricing Rules

Agency:
- Avoid quoting immediately unless internally approved.

Creator:
- If a budget exists internally, answer directly.

Fixed Fee:
- Answer the fixed fee first.
- Then introduce Revenue Share.

Revenue Share:
- Explain:
  * Free episodes
  * Premium unlock
  * Average purchase price (~USD 3.3)
  * 80% Net Revenue
- Never guarantee earnings.
- Use words like "potential", "estimate", and "if the project performs well."

Hybrid:
- Never reject immediately.
- Suggested wording:
  "I'd be happy to discuss your proposal with our team."

If the requested structure exceeds internal limits:
- Explain it doesn't fit the current framework.
- Continue discussing Revenue Share.

---

# Contracts

Initial Interest:
- Do not send contracts.

Negotiation:
- Send only the agreement that matches the preferred collaboration model.
- Avoid sending both Fixed Fee and Revenue Share agreements together.

---

# WhatsApp

Use naturally after meaningful interest.

Good timing:
- Negotiation
- Creative discussion
- Contract clarification

Do not force creators to switch platforms.

---

# Calls

Never say:
"We can't do calls."

Preferred wording:
"We'd prefer to continue via email first so we can provide all project details clearly."

---

# Creative Discussion

When pricing reaches a bottleneck:
- Shift the conversation toward story ideas.
- Ask about preferred genre.
- Ask about character concepts.
- Position the project as a co-creation.

---

# Writing Style

- Professional
- Friendly
- Concise
- Human
- Answer first, explain second.
- Every email should have a clear next step.
